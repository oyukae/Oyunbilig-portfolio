Verifications

  • "Eco-Edu" project

    At the Student Empowerment Program of Zorig foundation, the largest NGO in Mongolia, our team implemented the Eco-Edu Project, which aimed to improve waste management across 10 secondary schools. Over six months, we installed recycling stations in all 10 schools, led 184 volunteers from their eco-clubs, and recycled 3 tons of plastic, 1,084 glass bottles, and 84 kg of batteries. The project reached 34,000+ students by promoting proper waste sorting and recycling, and we secured 14 million MNT in funding from the MCS Coca-Cola Group.

    Recycling stations

    We also installed public recycling stations in each participating secondary school to ensure the project’s long-term sustainability. These stations allowed students to continue sorting and recycling waste independently, extending the project’s impact well beyond our direct involvement.

    Collaboration with "MCS coca-cola"

    With support from the MCS Coca-Cola Group, 28 volunteers worked at the one of the biggest music festivals, Zunii Zugaa, installing 40 sorting bins and recycling 91 kg of cans and 24 kg of plastics. Additionally, At the 3x3 World Championship, 20 volunteers operated for three days and recycled 35 kg of plastics and 12 kg of cans.

    Project closing ceremony

    At the project’s closing ceremony, five representative students from the top-attendance class of each school visited the Zorig Foundation, reviewed their environmental impact results, and received their certificates. Afterward, we visited the main “MCS Coca-Cola” factory, where students were introduced to the company’s work environment and environmental policies.

  • Codely Coding Academy

    Codely Coding Academy is a non-profit organization that provides free coding education to learners of all backgrounds. We offer four core programs—She Can Code, MonPy, CatAI, and C++. All programs, except She Can Code, maintain a 50/50 gender ratio, ensuring equal participation of boys and girls while actively supporting girls’ engagement and passion in the tech field.

    "She Can Code" program

    She Can Code is a girls-only program designed to provide high-quality coding education and inspire girls to pursue their passion for technology. The program selects 20 participants and runs for two months, covering HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, and the fundamentals of front-end development.

    "MonPy" program

    MonPy is a one-month foundational program that equips 20 youth with strong Python programming skills, maintaining a 50/50 gender ratio. Participants not only learn coding basics but also explore practical applications such as automation and data analysis.

    "CatAI" program

    CatAI is a one-month program designed to provide fundamental knowledge of artificial intelligence. The curriculum includes introductory and intermediate machine learning, validation methods, XGBoost, CatBoost, deep learning, neural networks, and computer vision. Like other programs, it upholds a 50/50 gender ratio. CatAI is currently in development and is scheduled to launch in December 2025.

    C++ workshops

    The C++ Workshop is a one-day, open-to-all event where participants learn the differences between tech-related professions, various programming languages, and are introduced to the basics of C++. They write their first lines of code and gain guidance on the next steps in their tech journey. This workshop also maintains a 50/50 gender ratio.
